# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small-to-mid business owners who have outgrown DIY marketing but can't justify an in-house team. Local service businesses, e-commerce, professional services. They arrive skeptical (burned by agencies before), busy, and non-technical. The job to be done: "take marketing off my plate and prove it's working."

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Sun Digital Marketing (est. 2009, Savannah GA + NJ). Sells the agency as "the outsourced marketing department," differentiated by SunSuite (38 proprietary AI tools) and Sol (the AI layer that works the overnight shift). Success = qualified strategy-call bookings via Calendly, filtered through the Sol chatbot which screens out vendors and solicitors.

## Brand Personality

Confident, warm, plain-spoken. Two founders who built the thing themselves — expert but never corporate. Emotional goals: confidence and trust ("my marketing is safe with them") plus genuine tech credibility ("they built their own AI platform"). Copy sounds like a smart person, not a brochure.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI startup: purple gradients, sci-fi vibes, buzzword soup, faceless tech
- Cheap local agency: stock photos, dated templates, "we do everything" clutter
- Corporate enterprise: stiff, cold, jargon-heavy, built for Fortune 500 not small business
- Salesy/pushy: popups, countdown timers, aggressive CTAs everywhere

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach** — an AI marketing agency's site must itself demonstrate craft (the live Sol demo, the dashboard mock, the AI chatbot ARE the pitch).
2. **Warm sun, not cold tech** — the orange sunburst gradient carries the AI story; never drift toward the purple/neon AI-startup palette.
3. **Show the humans** — two real founders, real photos, a real Google review. Trust comes from people, not claims.
4. **Plain English wins** — every headline and tool description readable by a non-technical business owner.
5. **One path to conversion** — everything funnels to the strategy call; no competing CTAs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Formal WCAG 2.1 AA target. Existing: skip link, focus-visible rings, ARIA roles/labels on nav and chat, aria-hidden on decorative elements. Hold new work to AA contrast (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text) and provide reduced-motion alternatives for reveal/typing animations.
